# Proposal Requirements

Students must submit the final proposal as `proposal.pdf`.

## Format

- Maximum 3 pages, not counting references or appendices.
- 11-point font, using a common legible typeface.
- 1-inch margins on all sides.
- Save the final proposal as a single PDF.

## Required Sections

1. Project title
2. Abstract
3. Keywords
4. Introduction: motivation, gap, and relevant prior work or source notes
5. Project goal
6. Methods: technical approach and agent workflow
7. Figure or diagram with caption
8. Expected results and research milestones with timeline estimates
9. Evaluation plan
10. Risks and mitigation
11. Resources, tools, budget, or release plan
12. References, assumptions, or source notes

## Notes For This Course Project

- The final artifact should read like a research proposal, not a short course implementation report.
- The course submission schedule is separate from the research timeline described in the proposal.
- The agent workflow must be specific: stages, inputs, outputs, feedback loop, revision loop, and stopping criteria.
- The figure should explain the proposed workflow, system architecture, study design, or evaluation setup. It should not be a decorative image.
- Unsupported claims should be marked as assumptions or connected to a source.
- Funding-agency fields such as PI, co-PI, institutional contact, cash funding, or cloud-credit requests are not required for this course project.

## Reference Style

The provided ARA template is the required course reference for format and section structure. Students may also study external proposal and fellowship examples to improve their workflow design. Useful patterns include:

- NSF-style review questions: what will be done, why it matters, how it will be done, how success will be measured, and what broader value the work may create.
- Fellowship-style research plans: clear research direction, innovative concept, relation to prior work, robustness, and potential impact.
- Grant-style expected results: concrete outcomes, milestones, artifacts, release plans, and realistic risks.

Suggested external references:

- [NSF GRFP solicitation](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program/nsf24-591/solicitation): intellectual merit, broader impacts, and research plan review questions.
- [Google PhD Fellowship](https://research.google/programs-and-events/phd-fellowship/): proposal strength, research impact, innovation, robustness, and future research direction.
- [Apple Scholars](https://www.apple.com/education/higher-education/scholars/): examples of fellowship-level research areas and research leadership expectations.
- [Civio](https://www.civio.ai/): example of a startup-style proposal/compliance agent product.
